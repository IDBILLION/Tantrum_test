Types of estate finance or mortgage Loan

There are only 3 types of estate finance or mortgage loan.

  1. Short-term Estate finance or mortage loan: It's usually advance from 0-5 years. It's also called BREEDING FINANCE. This loan is use in the interim before permanent fund to finance the project is source. It's usually provided by the commercial banks, Insurance companies or micro-finance bank. Short term finance can be guarantee by other institution who is ready to lend on long-term. The interest on short-term loan is always high.

    Other sources of short-term loan include boroowing from friends,relative or money lender.

  2. Medium Estate finance or mortage Loan: This loan is extended upto 10 year and can be source from any but most times commercial banks does not favour medium-term loan. In Nigeria mediun-term loan can be source from specialized banks.

    Precaution to be taken before signing a loan agreement.

  1. Don't agree to pay the loan back on specials days that are meant for public holidays.
  2. Don't be in a hurry to sign a Mortgage agreement "DEAL IS DEAL".
  3. Always be sure of your forecast of how to pay back your loan. Loan defaulters are criminals.
  4. Check the interest rate if it's not too high and if it's you can negotiate or go for higher amount with same interest rate.
  5. Always consent two or three mortgage loan before considering one

When applying for a loan there are some obligation the borrower and lender must agree on. This is call MORTGAGE DEED

This Include the following

  1. Party names, address of both the mortgagor and mortgagee
  2. Loan amount
  3. Repayment clause and mode of repayment.
  4. Interest rate clause either to fix or varies
  5. Description of the property
  6. Priorty of the mortage to use i.e First year, Second year or third year
  7. Prepayment Clause
  8. Due on sale Clause
  9. Escrow Provision
  10. Condition of the property
  11. Default clause
